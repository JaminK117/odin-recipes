Baklava

Photo by Rosa Stone (CC0 1.0)

Description

Crisp, buttery layers of golden filo meet a luscious filling of spiced nuts & sweet syrup in this classic baklava.
Each bite is a perfect harmony of crunch & melt, rich & aromatic. Whether served as a show-stopping dessert or a little indulgence with coffee, baklava is pure decadence.

Ingredients

  • 375g (3 3/4 cups) walnuts
  • 200g (1 1/4 cups) blanched whole almonds
  • 1/3 cup caster sugar
  • 3/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 125g unsalted butter, melted
  • 375g packet filo pastry
  • Ground pistachios for garnish

Method

  1. Prepare baklava syrup
  2. In a food processor, blitz walnuts until finely chopped. Transfer to large bowl.
    *Note: Do not over-process nuts as they will become oily
  3. Repeat with almonds, transferring in to the same bowl
  4. Add sugar & cinnamon to nut mixture. Combine well
  5. Brush the base and sides of an 18x28x3.5cm pan with the melted butter
  6. Unroll filo on to a large chopping board, placing the pan on top
  7. Cut the filo around the pan. Discard the trimmings
  8. Using 1 sheet of filo at a time, brush with butter and place in pan. Create 9 layers of filo
  9. Evenly scatter half the nut mixture on top of filo
  10. Repeat the buttering & layering of the filo for another 6 sheets, placing them over the nut mixture
  11. Evenly scatter the remaining nut mixture on top of filo
  12. Repeat the buttering & layering of a final 6 sheets of filo, laying them on top of the nut mixture. Using your hands, press the baklava to slightly compress
  13. Brush the top well with butter
  14. Preheat oven to 160c (fan-forced). Place the baklava in freezer for 10 minutes
  15. Lightly score the top few layers of the baklava in to diamond shapes
  16. Bake for 35 minutes. If the pastry is over-browning, cover with baking paper
  17. Pour syrup over baklava as soon as removed from oven. Let stand for 2 hours or until syrup is absorbed and baklava is cool
  18. Cut baklava in to diamond pieces, cutting along score marks made earlier
  19. Garnish with ground pistachios
  20. Baklava will keep for up to 1 week, covered & placed in refrigerator
